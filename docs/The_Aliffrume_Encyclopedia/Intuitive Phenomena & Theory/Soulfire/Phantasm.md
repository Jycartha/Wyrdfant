# Phantasm
Also referred to as spirits, phantams are a curious form of life born of [soulfire](../Pure%20Phlogistics/Soulfire.md) and [ichor](Ichor.md), birthed from the aggregate [halo](../Pure%20Phlogistics/Soul.md#Halo) of [sentients](../../Natural%20Sciences/Biology/Sapient%20Species/Consciousness.md) coalesceing within an [impression](../Pure%20Phlogistics/Conscious%20Plane.md#Impressions) around a central concept; the focal points responsible are often referred to as their **ideal**, while their area is referred to as their **dominion**. 

## By Structure
### Lesser Phantasm
The lowest class of phantasm, with barely a distinctive identity to perpetuate. More a loose collection of soulfire than creature, wisps permeate the environment wherever life abounds. As with all phantasms, their behavior depends upon their ideal. 

Many lesser phantasms are formed around nature, and will thus tend to stimulate plant growth, dew miscellany surfaces, create small gusts of wind, among other things. Such phantasms will often align with the primary ideal of their environment; those formed in a canyon generally thought to be windy will often habitually produce wind, those in a lake rumored to have treasure might occasionally sparkle like gold, etc.

### Greater Phantasm
A phantasm which possesses a [soul](../Pure%20Phlogistics/Soul.md), granting them sapience and a great deal more freedom.  However, greater phantasms are still bound within their impression, and are reliant upon it for survival. Without one, [like any other sapient mortal](../Pure%20Phlogistics/Soul.md#Death), they will slowly erode into the background until it either completely fades or regains its impression. 

### Ideal Phantasm
The highest classification of phantasm, which are those that are [phlogistically immortal](../Firekeeping/Phlogistic%20Immortality.md). Notably, this allows them to be unrestrained by the impression which birthed them, and makes them exceedingly difficult to cleanse. 

This makes ideal [shades](Shade.md) some of the most dangerous entities on the planet, as they often become the downfall of entire regions. As the shade cannot be normally quelled, this leaves pacifying it as the only viable option to contain the threat. Oddly enough, while [somagenics](Shade.md) are often more powerful than [phlogenics](Shade.md), ideal somagenics are often easer to subdue as their mental state is more readily understood, and thus more easily tempered. Phlogenics, meanwhile, can be entirely unpredictable, leading them to be difficult to subdue quickly and thus a greater long-term threat even if they're less powerful.

## By Origin
Phantasms are often further classified by their origin, listed below:
### Fairy
Harmonic phantasms born from natural coalescence, often as wisps. Uniquely, each classification of fairy has a colloquial name:
- Lesser fairies are "sprites"
- Greater fairies are "fey"
- Ideal fairies are "archfey"

### Fellsoul
Greater phantasms which have undergone ego death and are thus sustained purely, and transformed into shades. Given their possesion of a soul and memory accumulation, they are often more powerful then their nascent counterparts, but are also often easier to "pacify" by re-establishing the ego.

### Nightspawn
Shades formed from malicious coalescence
